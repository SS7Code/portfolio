About

I research numerical methods for fracture mechanics, focusing on the Virtual Element Method (VEM) and its application to phase-field fracture simulations and cohesive interface models. I implement prototypes in Abaqus (UEL/UMAT) and MATLAB, and I am interested in turning research code into reproducible tools.

Selected projects

VEM for phase-field fracture

Implementation of lowest-order VEM with phase-field fracture; studies on mesh transition and hanging nodes. (Paper in preparation / CMAME submission.)

Abaqus UEL: Virtual elements

Custom UEL for virtual elements supporting non-matching meshes and cohesive interfaces. Includes MATLAB pre/post-processing scripts.

Prototype CAE plugin

Designing a plugin to streamline fracture simulations — goal: reproducible workflows and improved pre/post-processing for industry users.
